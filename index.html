Project Information

In any economy, the healthcare sector is essential to the overall economy. Increasing the digital footprint of the essential information being engineered is a common task for IT experts in light of recent developments in the field. This is so that they may better serve the many stakeholders. Despite the fact that the health industry has been one of the most targeted areas in recent years, there are currently various solutions onboarded connecting hospitals, personnel, and patients. Both the research and the proposed system show how urgent it is to replace the existing solutions' inadequate security with more modern, well-tested methods.

Electronic health records (EHR) are a convenient alternative to paper medical records that allow patients to gain electronic access to their previous medical history. In this approach, patients are given the tools they need to take charge of their own electronic health records (EHRs), from the point of view of management and production to the point of control and dissemination to loved ones, healthcare providers, and other individuals with a legitimate need for access to this data. These electronic health records (EHRs) are also accessible to health care analysts and other service providers. Furthermore, patients can share their electronic health records (EHRs) with numerous parties during their Lifespan, allowing them to transfer from one doctor to another.

Blockchain refers to a distributed ledger of records kept and recorded by a network of peers rather than a single authority. This technology ensures the security of sensitive information while facilitating its safe transfer across networks. Through the network, everyone can store and share data, and all interactions are recorded. This technology can integrate many networks to provide personalized medical information. As a result, Blockchain is reliable and unchangeable. The field of healthcare is making great strides forward. Safeguarding information, facilitating its exchange, and facilitating its interoperability are all crucial concerns in population health management. Security, data transfer, interoperability, integrity, and real-time updating and access are all bolstered by this technology if it is applied correctly. Blockchain technology provides a secure method for patients and medical professionals to store, transfer, and consult data online.

A person or group going by the name Satoshi Nakamoto invented the blockchain system. The double-spending problem was solved independently of any central authority or trusted third party with the advent of Bitcoin's blockchain. There has been a recent uptick in the prevalence of diseases and conditions affecting the senior population. Unfortunately, older patients with health issues are still monitored and treated using the same old methods

Before the advent of smart contracts on the blockchain, the main issues in Electronic Health Record (EHR) Management revolved around whether or not to use cloud infrastructures or local centralized systems to store and share EHRs. A recent development in IT is the use of cloud computing to deliver healthcare services. Because of these centralized systems, hospitals and other healthcare providers had to keep data in locally administered structures and databases. Centralized EHR management solutions, however, have a number of limitations that are described in detail, such as a lack of transparency, no patient control, inefficient data sharing, and restricted system compatibility.

Health care systems, both public and private, stand to benefit greatly from the application of digital health technologies, which have the potential to vastly enhance efficiency, effectiveness, and the quality of service. The Ministry of Health of Sri Lanka recognized the importance of digital health in 2016 by publishing the National eHealth Guidelines and Standards with the goal of achieving uniformity in the implementation of eHealth solutions in Sri Lanka while protecting the rights of patients and the quality of their care. Due to advancements in the information technology sector, Blockchain is now available as Digital Health gains acceptance.

Access control systems are put in place to prevent unauthorized users from gaining entry to sensitive system resources. The increasing development of information systems has increased the demand for more advanced access control approaches that address the centralization, scalability, and trust challenges that plague conventional mechanisms. Because of its high trust and tamper-resistance, blockchain technology can be a feasible alternative for imposing access control limits as a decentralized, distributed solution. This paper discusses the development of blockchain access control systems and examines their features, components, designs, and model support.

In the era of eHealth, numerous devices, including wearables, sensors, and imaging equipment, can contribute data to a patient's Electronic Health Records (EHRs). The volume of electronic health records (EHR) data was projected to rise to about 2,314 zettabytes by 2020, exceeding this estimate by 48 percent annually. More than 176,709,305 patient records were compromised as a result of more than 2,181 healthcare data breaches that occurred between 2009 and 2017. Recently, blockchain has been advocated as a replacement to the current methods used for managing electronic health records (EHR). Since blockchain technology is inherently secure, it can be used to keep unchangeable logs of EHR access events. A consensus mechanism checks and verifies each access event before adding it to the blockchain.

Technology advancements like blockchain and cryptography have made it possible to upgrade digital archives and property rights. When it comes to digital assets like music files, video files, and domain names, a Non-Fungible Token (NFT) serves as a verifiable proof of ownership that cannot be transferred via the blockchain.

In addition, NFTs advocate for the openness of data transfers, stringent zero-knowledge for high trust and immutability, and nearly faultless data transmission across many platforms.

Even in its preliminary phases of worldwide application, NFTs is proving its efficacy and high security in the provision of essential services. Tokens issued by NFT can be used to confirm a person's identity in conjunction with their personal medical record. When it comes to sharing private medical information with healthcare providers or healthcare finance services, NFTs can be utilized as a robust alternative to protect patients' privacy.

A critical part of any hospital's infrastructure, medical laboratories provide invaluable diagnostic information. Poor processes created for preserving and sharing medical reports have been linked to multiple occasions when lab results were disclosed. This leak was caused by poor authentication in download portals; thus hospitals are now storing patent lab data in a different location where they can be accessed by third parties. Invoice identifiers and codes are downloaded by them. A patient's medical records are confidential and should not be discussed in any setting without their express permission. Such exceptions can constitute a violation of the HIPPA policy rule]. A safe method of exchanging electronic health records is not yet provided for in the infrastructure that is in place.

The fact that there are various kinds of laboratory tests, each with its own standard report style, is also cause for concern. It would be helpful if the report download link could adapt its report format depending on the laboratory analysis. Because of this, most online health laboratory systems only offer a subset of available lab test findings for download. That's a known flaw in the online world, so be careful. Reports cannot be accessed online, therefore patients must physically visit the lab to receive them. Documents were increasingly being stored and shared digitally rather than physically. The absence of integrity, secrecy, and nonrepudiation of the digital report makes it impossible to do so with documents containing health information. Not possible to take the aforementioned precautions when using email, WhatsApp, etc. A weakness in the healthcare system has occurred here.

The suggested system may guarantee the document's authenticity and non-repudiation by using a robust authentication system, a dynamic report template that generates a portal, and a QR-based digital signature mechanism. To Implement a security resilient system to secure Electronic Health Records using the blockchain technology accounting for the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of patient data

The goal of this initiative is to safeguard patients' personal information, medical history, and other EHR components by implementing blockchain technology. The author advocates using smart contracts, which are dynamic between actors in the system, to accomplish this goal. The goal is to use a model that incorporates JSON files to streamline transactions and strengthen access control mechanisms in EHR management settings that rely on blockchain technology. The proposed system uses Know Your Customer checks, multi-factor authentication, and a QR code-based signing method to generate secure reports. NFT-based initial authentication is performed in accordance with HIPAA requirements to safeguard patient identify and privacy.
